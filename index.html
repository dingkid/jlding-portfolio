About Me:

I'm Jessica "Jay" Ding, a fourth year (out of five) majoring in mechanical engineering and minoring in mathematics at Northeastern University. Currently at Northeastern, I am a part of the Combat Robotics Team and Digital Illustration Association.
My decision to become an engineer stems from my interest in math and physics in high school. Growing up as mainly an artist, going into engineering may have seemed drastic, but I enjoy seeing how the two disciplines overlap and how skills from one can be applied to another. Having done two co-ops, I have gained valuable skills in documentation, working with a team, and generally being comfortable in an engineering work environment. I know that I would like my future work to be hands-on. My dream right now would be to work on public transit. I'm still trying to find exactly what I want to do in the future and am open to all experiences in the field. Ultimately, I would like to design things that help the every day person like me and make the world a more accessible place.
I am a pretty avid violist. In high school. I played viola in the Boston Youth Symphony and Boston Philharmonic Youth Orchestra. In college, I've performed in a chamber group and as first stand violist in the NU symphony orchestra. In my freetime, I enjoy making digital artwork, knitting, playing rhythm games, inline skating, skiing/snowboarding, and riding my longboard poorly around the city.
